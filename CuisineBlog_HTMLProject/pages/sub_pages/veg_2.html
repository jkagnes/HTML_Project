Bhatura Recipe

Ingredients

    For Bhatura:

  • Refined flour (maida) 2½ cups
  • Yogurt ½ cup
  • Baking powder ½ tsp
  • Soda bicarbonate a pinch
  • Salt 1 tsp
  • Sugar 2 tsp
  • Oil for deep fry

    • For Chole:

  • White chickpeas 1 cup
  • Channa masala powder 2 tbsp
  • Oil 3 tbsp
  • Mustard seed ½ tsp
  • Bay leaf 1 small
  • Onion ⅓ cup chopped
  • Ginger-garlic paste 1 tsp
  • Turmeric power ¼ tsp
  • Asafoetida a pinch
  • Curry leaves few
  • Tomato ½ cup chopped
  • Green chilies 1 or 2 slit
  • Coriander leaves 2 tbsp chopped

Method of Preparation

    For Bhatura:

  • Place flour and baking powder, soda bicarbonate and salt in a bowl. Mix well and pass it through a sieve. Mix yogurt with salt and sugar.
  • Add this to the flour and add about a cup of water and mix gradually to make a soft dough by light kneading.
  • Incorporate two tablespoons of oil into the dough and cover the dough with a wet cloth. Keep it aside for an hour.
  • Divide dough into sixteen equal portions. Roll them into balls. Cover and keep to ferment for ten minutes. Grease your palms with a little oil and flatten the balls.
  • Roll out into five-inch diameter diskettes. Heat sufficient oil in a kadai and deep fry the bhaturas on high heat till light brown on both sides. Drain on absorbent paper. Serve hot with chole.

    • For Chole:

  • Rinse the 1 cup chana/dried chickpeas for a couple of times in water. then soak the chickpeas in enough water overnight or for 8 to 9 hours.
  • Drain the water, rinse the chickpeas and pressure cook with ½ tsp salt, 3 cups water, for 2 to 3 whistles on medium to high flame.
  • Heat 3 tbsp oil in a pan, add ½ tsp mustard seeds and allow them to crackle, then add bay leaf and stir.
  • Add ⅓ cup chopped onion, stir and saute till the onions turn translucent and soften.
  • Then add 1 tsp ginger-garlic paste, ¼ tsp turmeric power, a pinch of asafoetida and few curry leaves.
  • Stir and saute till the raw aroma of ginger-garlic disappears.
  • Add ½ cup chopped tomato. saute for about 2 to 3 minutes till the tomatoes soften.
  • Add the Channa masala powder, stir very well.
  • Then add the drained chanas. stir again well and saute for a minute and required amount of water and 1 or 2 slit green chilies.
  • Allow to boil and then simmer for 5-7 minutes on a low to medium flame or more till the gravy thickens.
  • Switch off the flame add 2 tbsp chopped coriander leaves.
